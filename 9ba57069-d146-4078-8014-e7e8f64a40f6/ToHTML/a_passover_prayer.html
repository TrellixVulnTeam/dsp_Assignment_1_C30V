Our story this night tells of the transformative 
moment when we, a cohort of cousins, united as a 
people, forever linked in faith and infate. We walked 
as one between the birthing waters of the Sea to be 
reconstituted as Am Yisrael— the people of Israel.

So we sing Dayeynu – had God only done that 
and no more, it would have been enough.

Together at Sinai we stood and received Torah, the 
gift of God’s love. As women and men now free, we 
entered into a covenant of mutual obligation and 
inter-dependence, responsible to the Holy One and 
to each other.

Dayenu – had God only done that and no more, 
it would have been enough.

That covenant binds us still, transcending time and 
place, linking each Jew to every Jew.

Each one, dependent on all.

Kol Yisrael areyvim zeh bazeh.

We are responsible for each other. We are  
responsible to each other.

Seated here, as well as at seder tables around the 
world, are loved ones and friends, shoulder to  
shoulder with all who sing the same songs of  
salvation and praise, family all.

The Israeli Jew and the Iraqi Jew, the  
Argentinean Jew and the American Jew, the  
traditional Jew and the unconventional Jew,

The Russian grandparent and the Ethiopian child, 
those of substance and those on subsistence.

We sit as one at this Seder, differences dissolving 
in common history and hope and in deepened 
awareness of our mutual dependency.

We are so much stronger as a family than as individuals.

For our collective responsibility - at home, in 
Israel and around the world - is monumental  
and compelling.

All Israel is responsible, one to the other.

Kol Yisrael areyvim zeh bazeh.

And for this, there is no Dayenu. There can be  
no “enough.”

For as much as we do, there is more to be done.

by Rabbi Donald B. Rossoff,  
Chair, Rabbinic Cabinet of MetroWest NJ
